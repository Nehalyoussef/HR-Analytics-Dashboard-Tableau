## Project Overview
This project presents an **interactive HR analytics dashboard** focused on analyzing **employee attrition, workforce demographics, and job satisfaction** across departments, education fields, gender, and age groups.

The dashboard is designed to help HR teams and decision-makers **identify retention risks**, understand employee sentiment, and support **data-driven workforce planning and engagement strategies**.

## Tools Used
- Data Visualization Tool (Dashboard-based analysis)
- Excel / BI-style reporting concepts
- Aggregated HR employee data

## Dataset Description
- **Total employees:** 1,470  
- **Attrition count:** 237  
- **Overall attrition rate:** 16.12%  
- **Key attributes include:**
  - Department
  - Job role
  - Age and age groups
  - Gender
  - Education field
  - Job satisfaction rating
  - Attrition status

## Dashboard Components & Analysis

### Key HR KPIs
- Total Employee Count
- Active Employees
- Attrition Count & Attrition Rate
- Average Employee Age
- Attrition distribution by gender

These KPIs provide a **high-level snapshot** of workforce health and turnover risk.

### Department-Level Attrition
- Sales department shows the **highest attrition contribution**
- R&D and HR follow with significantly lower attrition rates
- Enables HR to prioritize **department-specific retention strategies**

### Employee Distribution by Age Group
- Workforce is concentrated between **ages 25â€“44**
- Attrition patterns vary noticeably across age bands
- Helps identify **early-career vs mid-career retention challenges**

### Job Satisfaction Analysis
- Job satisfaction ratings (1â€“4) analyzed by job role
- Sales Executives and Research Scientists show **higher dissatisfaction counts**
- Supports targeted engagement and role-specific improvement plans

### Education Field & Attrition
- Highest attrition observed among employees from:
  - Life Sciences
  - Medical backgrounds
- Suggests a need to review:
  - Role alignment
  - Career growth opportunities
  - Workload expectations for these groups
    
### Attrition by Gender & Age Group
- Gender-based attrition patterns vary by age group
- Younger age groups show **higher attrition proportions**
- Insightful for designing:
  - Early-career engagement programs
  - Gender-inclusive retention initiatives

## Key Insights
- Overall attrition rate of **16.12%** signals moderate retention risk
- Sales roles and early-career employees require immediate attention
- Job satisfaction strongly correlates with attrition patterns
- Education background plays a noticeable role in employee turnover
- Gender and age segmentation reveals non-uniform attrition behavior
